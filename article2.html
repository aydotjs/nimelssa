CITY OF GLOOM

Abdulmueed Balogun.

  • In this city, there are boys praying never to morph into men, never to cross the
  • border of boyhood, after beholding the hells their fathers burnt in before fetching
  • them a dreg of putrid milk to quench their blazing thirst. In this city of ours, there are children whose
  • mouths become a stage of dance for dirges every time the sun walks the sky like a tyrant, every time a
  • stranger asks after their fathers, their mothers, their brothers and their sisters. In this city of gloom, every heart bears testaments
  • to pain and agony, every face-- a mystery longing to be resolved. In this city tonight, there are men your father's age, girl's
  • your daughter's names, boys sharing similar dreams with you, women your mother's portrait, praying as they go to sleep, never to embrace the light of dawn.

HORSE OF TIME

Abdulmueed Balogun

  • Ride, ride, ride slowly, the horse of time. When the wave
  • of life rages like a tyrant, even when the brilliant sky mourns
  • the departure of light like a mother bereft of her only son,
  • still slow, slow and slowly like a gentle snail
  • ride the horse of time. It won't elude you, what's yours, like a
  • sneeze from your nose, stars do not deprive the night of their
  • blessings even after a bloody war. Hurry, hurry, hurry, hurrying
  • like racing clouds, still you will never match the giant
  • pace of time. It won't elude you, what's yours, the moon
  • do not inflict the night with blindness even after a bloody coup.
