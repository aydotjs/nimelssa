HEALTH WEEK AND PAYMENT OF DUES

THE 2ND NIMELSSA PRESS CONFERENCE

The 2nd NIMELSSA PRESS CONFRENCE officially started at 7:25pm on the 6th of November, 2021. The press conference was called in order to discuss the issues of payment of dues and the HEALTH WEEK.

The President, Mr Oyebamiji Toba was questioned with reference to the conclusion of the last congress about the setting up of a committee consisting of NIMELSSAITES who have paid their dues in order to encourage students who are yet to make the payments. He stated that the committee has been created and the link would be made available to NIMELSSAITES as soon as possible. The committee is going to consist of two(2) members from each class who will be the representatives of their class and decide along with their classmates what the packages for this session will be.

Mr President also mentioned that the HEALTH WEEK will hold although less than 50% of NIMELSSAITES have paid up the required dues. He laid emphasis on how discouraging this is and pleaded with NIMELSSAITES; those present and otherwise, to pay the dues as quickly as possible in order for the HEALTH WEEK to be successful as planned.

A proposed date for the HEALTH WEEK has been set putting into consideration mainly the forthcoming examinations. It is going to be the traditional week-long program that kicks off on the 13th of December. Committees have been set up and will be made open to NIMELSSAITES very soon. Although the HEALTH WEEK is going to feature the traditional programs; bonfire night, symposium, CHAP, games/sports, variety night and the dinner, it is going to be the “talk of the century”, the President promised.

A consistent ability to blow the minds of NIMELSSAITES is a characteristic of this tenure and Mr Oyebamiji has promised that there is more to come. He hinted that there are a couple of things lined up before the end of this tenure and asked NIMELSSAITES to look forward to it.

It is worthy of note that NO clinical student will be allowed to participate in the forthcoming examinations without evidence of the payment of dues. As implied by the President in his closing remark, an unimpressive number of clinical students have payed the required dues. He urged the clinical students to satisfy all the requirements in order to be eligible to sit for the examinations, emphasizing on the payment of dues. He also mentioned the e-Magazine that is going to be the first of its kind in this department and implored NIMELSSAITES to send in their pieces which include articles, cartoon, poems, short stories and so on. The deadline for submission of articles is the 15th of November, 2021. “ It will be a great priviledge if your article is published in the first ever magazine that is coming out of this department” he said. With 25 NIMELSSAITES in attendance, including the members of the executive, the press conference came to an end at 7:50pm.
