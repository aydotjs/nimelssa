History Of The Department

HISTORY OF THE DEPARTMENT

The University of Ibadan started a B.SC. Medical Laboratory Technology (B.Sc. Med. Lab. Tech.) Programme the 1967/68 session. Over the period, the national need for this category of health professionals was met by graduates of the School of Medical Laboratory Technology of some Teaching Hospitals in the country. However, to cope with advances in science and medicine, the Medical Laboratory Science Council of Nigeria (MLSCN) stopped the Diploma prog ramme about a decade ago and decided that the minimum qualification for registration with the councilshall be a professional degree in Medical Laboratory Sciences, the Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Science (BMLS) degree.

To prevent a gap in the human resource requirement for the health system in our country, the Executive Secretary of the National Universities Commission (NUC) and the Federal Ministry of Health constituted an inter-ministerial committee to resolve the problem. The committee recommended that the parent universities of the Teaching Hospitals that were running the Diploma programme sho be encouraged to establish degree programmes and get approval for them as fast as possible.

The Management of the University College Hospital, Ibadan through a letter of 30th March 2006 to the Dean of the Faculty of Basic Medical Sciences, College of Medicine, University of Ibadan formally requested for introduction of a degree programme in Medical Laboratory Science. The Faculty decided to commence the process of reactivation of the suspended degree programme of Bachelor of Sciences in Medical Laboratory Sciences (B.Sc. Med. Sci) as Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Sciences (BMLS).

The Faculty Board of Basic Medical Sciences considered the request and approved the proposal. The Faculty Board then set up a committee to review the accepted proposal and create a comprehensive curriculum for the Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Sciences programme. The curriculum was then approved sequentially by the Faculty Board, Academic Board of the College of Medicine, Senate Curriculum Committee and the University Senate. The National Universities Commission (NUC), after a Resource Verification Exercise subsequently approved the curriculum for the commencement of the programme in September 2012. Nine pioneering students were admitted for the 2012/2013 academic session. Afterwards, there have been five more intakes into the Department in the university.
